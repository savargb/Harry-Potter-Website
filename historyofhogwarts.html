The Founders & The Sorting Hat

Over a thousand years ago, the four greatest witches and wizards of the age—Godric Gryffindor, Helga Hufflepuff, Rowena Ravenclaw, and Salazar Slytherin—pooled their magic to build Hogwarts. To ensure students were placed in the right houses after they were gone, Gryffindor enchanted his own hat with the combined intelligence of all four founders.

The Moving Staircases

The castle is famous for its 142 staircases. They range from grand marble flights to narrow, winding ones. Most remarkably, they move on their own, often changing destination while students are still on them. This sentient architecture is part of the castle's ancient defense system.

"There are also doors that wouldn't open unless you asked them politely, or tickled them in exactly the right place."

The Chamber of Secrets

Salazar Slytherin left the school after a dispute regarding the admission of Muggle-born students. Before leaving, he secretly constructed a hidden chamber. According to legend, only the Heir of Slytherin can open the entrance (hidden in a girls' bathroom) and control the ancient Basilisk dwelling within.

The Enchanted Ceiling

The Great Hall serves as the heart of Hogwarts. Its ceiling is famously bewitched to mirror the actual sky outside, whether it be a clear starry night or a raging thunderstorm. Thousands of floating candles illuminate the hall during feasts, held aloft by permanent hovering charms.
