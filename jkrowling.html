J.K. Rowling

J.K. Rowling’s life is a famous story of how a great idea and hard work can change everything. Born in England in 1965, she loved writing even as a young child.

The idea for Harry Potter came to her suddenly in 1990 while she was sitting on a delayed train. She didn’t have a pen or paper at the time, so she spent the four-hour ride dreaming up the details of a magical school and a boy wizard.

The next few years were very difficult for her. After living abroad and facing personal hardships, she moved to Scotland as a struggling single mother. During this time, she sat in cafes while her daughter slept and finished the first Harry Potter book. She was rejected by 12 different publishers before a small company called Bloomsbury finally said "yes" in 1997.

Today, she is one of the most successful authors in history. Beyond the wizarding world, she writes popular detective stories under the name Robert Galbraith and spends much of her time giving to charities like Lumos.
