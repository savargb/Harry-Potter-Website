JK Rowling


J.K. Rowling’s life is a famous story of how a great idea and hard work can change everything. Born in England in 1965, she loved writing even as a young child. The idea for Harry Potter came to her suddenly in 1990 while she was sitting on a delayed train. She didn’t have a pen or paper at the time, so she spent the four-hour ride dreaming up the details of a magical school and a boy wizard.
The next few years were very difficult for her. She moved to Portugal, got married, had a daughter, and later got divorced. She moved to Scotland as a struggling single mother with very little money. During this time, she sat in cafes while her daughter slept and finished the first Harry Potter book. She was rejected by 12 different publishers before a small company called Bloomsbury finally said "yes" in 1997.
Once the books were published, they became a massive hit all over the world. J.K. Rowling went from having almost nothing to becoming one of the most successful authors in history. Her stories were turned into famous movies, theme parks, and toys. Today, she still writes popular detective stories under the secret name Robert Galbraith. While she is sometimes in the news for her strong opinions on social issues, she remains a huge part of modern culture and spends much of her time giving money to charities that help children and women.
