J.K. Rowling

A journey that began on a delayed train in 1990 changed the landscape of literature forever.

Born in England in 1965, Rowling was a dreamer from the start. The concept of Harry Potter emerged fully formed during a four-hour train delay. Without a pen, she spent the journey building the foundations of the Wizarding World in her mind.

Her path to success was not easy. Writing as a struggling single mother in Edinburgh cafes, she faced rejection from 12 different publishers before Bloomsbury finally took a chance on the boy who lived in 1997.
